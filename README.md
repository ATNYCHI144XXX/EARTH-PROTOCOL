### Continental Drift Timeline

**Rodinia (1.1 billion - 750 million years ago)**
- Supercontinent assembly during Proterozoic Eon
- Rifting initiated around 800 mya

**Pangaea (335 - 175 million years ago)**

- Peak configuration ~250 mya (Permian-Triassic boundary)
- Breakup began ~200 mya (Triassic-Jurassic)

**Gondwana & Laurasia (175 - 66 million years ago)**
- Gondwana: South America, Africa, Antarctica, Australia, India, Madagascar
- Laurasia: North America, Europe, Asia
- Separated by Tethys Ocean

**Present Configuration (66 mya - Present)**
- Atlantic Ocean formation and widening
- India-Asia collision creating Himalayas (~50 mya)
- Continuous movement: Atlantic widens ~2.5 cm/year, Pacific shrinks

**Future Projection (50-250 million years)**
- Predicted supercontinent "Pangaea Ultima" or "Amasia"
- Atlantic may begin closing; Pacific may close completely

## 3. GLOBAL Mass Extinction Events

The "Big Five" plus current crisis:

### 1. Ordovician-Silurian Extinction (444 million years ago)

**Casualties:** ~85% of marine species  
**Duration:** Two pulses over 1-2 million years  
**Causes:**
- Rapid glaciation and sea level drop
- Ocean anoxia (oxygen depletion)
- Climate cooling then rapid warming

**Victims:** Trilobites (many families), brachiopods, bryozoans, conodonts  
**Survivors:** Nautiloids, some brachiopods

### 2. Late Devonian Extinction (372 million years ago)

**Casualties:** ~75% of species  
**Duration:** Multiple pulses over 20 million years  
**Causes:**
- Ocean anoxia events
- Possible asteroid impacts
- Climate change and sea level fluctuations
- Plant colonization of land altering weathering

**Victims:** Reef-building organisms, armored fish (placoderms), many marine invertebrates  
**Survivors:** Sharks, lobe-finned fish (ancestors of tetrapods)

### 3. Permian-Triassic "Great Dying" (252 million years ago)

**Casualties:** ~96% of marine species, ~70% of terrestrial vertebrates  
**Duration:** 60,000 years (main event)  
**Causes:**
- Siberian Traps volcanic eruptions (1 million+ years of volcanism)
- Global warming (+10-15Â°C)
- Ocean acidification
- Hydrogen sulfide poisoning
- Ozone layer depletion

**Victims:** Trilobites (extinction), many therapsids, most marine life  
**Survivors:** Lystrosaurus (dominated early Triassic), ancestors of dinosaurs and mammals

## 9. GLOBAL Survival & Preparedness

### Region-Specific Disaster Preparedness

#### Pacific Ring of Fire Nations

**Earthquake & Tsunami Preparedness:**
- Maintain 72-hour emergency kit (water, food, medical supplies)
- Know evacuation routes to high ground (>30 m elevation for tsunami-prone coasts)
- Secure heavy furniture and appliances
- Practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" drills
- Install early warning apps (e.g., JMA Earthquake Early Warning - Japan, ShakeAlert - USA)

**Countries:** Japan, Indonesia, Philippines, Chile, Peru, Ecuador, USA (West Coast), Canada (BC), Mexico, New Zealand

#### Tropical Cyclone Belt Regions

**Hurricane/Typhoon/Cyclone Preparedness:**
- Monitor forecasts during cyclone season
- Prepare shutters or plywood for windows
- Stock 7-14 days of non-perishable food and water
- Secure outdoor items that could become projectiles
- Know evacuation zones (especially for storm surge)
- Have backup power for medical devices

**Regions:** Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, Atlantic Coast USA, Philippines, Taiwan, Japan, Bay of Bengal, Northern Australia, Mozambique Channel

#### Flood-Prone River Valleys

**Flood Preparedness:**
- Understand your flood zone (100-year, 500-year floodplain)
- Elevate utilities (electrical panels, HVAC) above expected flood levels
- Install sump pumps and battery backups
- Keep sandbags or flood barriers ready
- Develop evacuation plan before rainy season
- Purchase flood insurance (often not covered by standard policies)

**Major Basins:** Mississippi (USA), Ganges-Brahmaputra (Bangladesh/India), Yellow River (China), Mekong (SE Asia), Amazon (Brazil)

#### Drought & Water-Scarce Regions

**Water Security Strategies:**
- Rainwater harvesting systems (where precipitation occurs)
- Efficient irrigation (drip systems)
- Drought-resistant crops and landscaping
- Water storage (minimum 2 weeks supply)
- Community wells and groundwater management
- Desalination access (coastal areas)

**Regions:** Sahel, Horn of Africa, Central Asia, Middle East, Southwest USA/Mexico, Australian interior

#### Volcanic Hazard Zones

**Volcanic Emergency Preparedness:**
- N95 masks or respirators (for ashfall)
- Goggles to protect eyes from ash
- Cover water sources during ashfall
- Know lahar (mudflow) paths if near stratovolcanoes
- Monitor volcanic alert levels
- Have evacuation plan for major eruptions

**High-Risk Populations:** Java (Indonesia), Luzon (Philippines), Kamchatka (Russia), Andes (South America), Naples region (Italy)

## ğŸ”¬ Scientific Principles & Laws

### Fundamental Earth Science Laws

**Principle of Uniformitarianism**
- "The present is the key to the past"
- Geological processes occurring today also occurred in the past
- Established by James Hutton (1788), refined by Charles Lyell (1830s)

**Law of Superposition**
- In undisturbed sedimentary layers, older layers are at the bottom
- Foundation of relative dating
- Established by Nicolaus Steno (1669)

**Law of Faunal Succession**
- Fossil organisms succeed each other in a definite and recognizable order
- Same fossils = same age globally
- William Smith (1799)

**Isostasy**
- Continental crust "floats" on denser mantle
- Explains mountains and ocean basins
- Explains post-glacial rebound

**Plate Tectonics Theory**
- Lithosphere divided into moving plates
- Driven by mantle convection
- Explains earthquakes, volcanoes, mountain building
- Synthesized 1960s-1970s (Wegener, Hess, Wilson, et al.)
